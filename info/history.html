The History and Evolution of The Big Chungtila

Around 200 million years ago, the dinosaurs roamed in the supercontinent called Pangea. Our ancestor Volaticotherium lived near the equation of the Earth. During this Jurassic period, the Pangea slowly began to break apart into seven continents today. The Volaticotherium split into two different animals due to its unique environment: Chungtila in South America and ground squirrel in Africa. When the Ice Age striked around one million years ago, the Volaticotherium started to develop thick fur to survive the cold temperatures.
